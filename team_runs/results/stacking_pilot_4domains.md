## 9. Contributions — interpretation per author

Each author has a defensible role supported by numbers in this report.

### Sonya (fintech fusion)
Standalone PR-AUC = 0.7284 (second among singletons), R@P≥0.9 = 0.1077 (second), ROC = 0.9522 (third). Strongest 4-way blends are anchored on a fusion-heavy weighting. Fusion provides a precision-tuned backbone whose R@P≥0.9 is competitive with Diana's at the operating point used for hard-precision auto-blocking. **Role: ensemble anchor; deployment candidate for high-precision automatic blocking.**

### Karina (mobile/ads CatBoost)
Standalone metrics are the lowest of the four (PR = 0.6562), but contributes **94 unique top-500 catches** — items with mobile/ads characteristics (typosquat patterns, app-like listings) that fintech and real-estate models under-rank. Removing karina (triple f+d+a) gives slightly higher PR-AUC point estimate (within CI) but loses these 94 unique catches. In every blend she participates in, her PR-AUC and ROC-AUC contributions are positive in the bootstrap. **Role: domain-specialization diversifier.**

### Diana (real estate M2 multimodal)
With the corrected probas, **Diana now leads singletons on PR-AUC (0.7375) and on R@P≥0.9 (0.1154)**. Her real-estate signal (CLIP-512 + K-means structural + TF-IDF SVD-50) is orthogonal to fintech and contributes the strongest pure-PR-AUC lift to the ensemble. Triple f+d+a (her, Sonya, Albina) is the highest-PR-AUC blend overall. **Role: PR-AUC and precision-tuned leader; central contributor across both PR and R@P axes.**

### Albina (4th domain)
Standalone ROC = 0.9545 (highest among singletons), PR-AUC = 0.7084 (third), R@P≥0.9 = 0.0276 (lowest). Contributes **133 unique top-500 catches — the highest of any single model.** Her ranking promotes items the other three under-rank. With Albina in the blend, the ensemble's R@P significantly improves over Karina and Albina herself, and matches Sonya/Diana. **Role: top-of-ranking diversifier; ROC-AUC leader among singletons.**

## 10. What we did not do (deliberate)

- **Trained meta-classifier (LR/LGBM over probas).** Requires per-domain probas on `team_val_idx.npy`. Team chose naive linear blending with bootstrap CIs as the shipped method.
- **Per-weight grid search optimised on test.** We hand-picked a small set of weights informed by previous pilots; bootstrap CI accounts for sampling variance but does not formally correct for blend selection.
- **Calibration (isotonic / Platt) before blending.** Marginal effect estimated at ~0.001 PR-AUC by Diana, below bootstrap CI width.
- **Per-domain breakdown of metrics.** Future work for individual chapters — measuring how each model performs specifically on its native-domain subset of the team test.

Cross-domain Pearson correlations sit in the 0.85 – 0.93 range, well below the ~0.98 we observe within a single author's model family, leaving substantial headroom for ensembling.
>
> The weighted blend **0.35·Sonya + 0.15·Karina + 0.30·Diana + 0.20·Albina** achieves PR-AUC = 0.7494, ROC-AUC = 0.9571, and Recall@P≥0.9 = 0.1176 on the team test split. Compared against each individual headline model with paired bootstrap (B = 1000, seed = 42), the ensemble shows the following gains:
>
> - vs **Sonya's fusion** (PR = 0.7284, ROC = 0.9522, R@P = 0.1077): ΔPR = **+2.10 pp** [+1.49; +2.68]\*, ΔROC = **+0.49 pp** [+0.40; +0.57]\*, ΔR@P = +1.31 pp [−4.17; +8.37] (n.s.)
> - vs **Karina** (PR = 0.6562, ROC = 0.9407, R@P = 0.0666): ΔPR = **+9.31 pp** [+8.58; +10.08]\*, ΔROC = **+1.63 pp** [+1.47; +1.81]\*, ΔR@P = **+6.08 pp** [+1.96; +12.20]\*
> - vs **Diana** (PR = 0.7375, ROC = 0.9484, R@P = 0.1154): ΔPR = **+1.19 pp** [+0.75; +1.65]\*, ΔROC = **+0.87 pp** [+0.73; +1.00]\*, ΔR@P = −0.34 pp [−5.46; +6.52] (n.s.)
> - vs **Albina** (PR = 0.7084, ROC = 0.9545, R@P = 0.0276): ΔPR = **+4.10 pp** [+3.38; +4.86]\*, ΔROC = **+0.25 pp** [+0.09; +0.42]\*, ΔR@P = **+9.26 pp** [+3.29; +14.57]\*
>
> The ensemble dominates every individual headline on PR-AUC and ROC-AUC with statistically significant CIs. On Recall@P≥0.9 the ensemble significantly improves Karina and Albina; for Sonya and Diana the difference is within sampling noise, confirming their roles as the strongest standalone models in the precision-tuned regime.
>
