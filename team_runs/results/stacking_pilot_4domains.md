## 9. Contributions — interpretation per author

Each author has a defensible role in the final ensemble, supported by the numbers in this report.

### Sonya (fintech fusion)
**Strongest single domain on PR-AUC and on Recall@P≥0.9** (the precision-tuned operating point). Every fusion-anchored blend beats every blend that omits fusion on R@P≥0.9. Fusion provides the precision-confident core that the other three diversify around. **Role: ensemble anchor.**

### Karina (mobile/ads CatBoost)
Lowest standalone metrics, but contributes **96 unique top-500 catches** that none of the other three find — items that share mobile/ads characteristics (typosquat patterns, app-like listings) which are under-ranked by fintech/real-estate models. In the bootstrap, every blend that includes karina is significant on PR-AUC; removing karina (triple_fda) drops PR-AUC by ~0.4 pp. **Role: domain diversifier.**

### Diana (real estate M2 multimodal)
Highest standalone ROC-AUC (0.9561), and the single biggest jump in pilot history: adding diana to a Sonya-only baseline raised the PR-AUC gain from +0.60 pp (with karina) to +1.46 pp (with karina + diana). Her real-estate signal (CLIP-512 + K-means structural + TF-IDF SVD-50) is orthogonal to fintech and contributes the strongest pure-PR-AUC lift. **Role: PR-AUC amplifier.**

### Albina
**Contributes the most unique top-500 catches of any single model (140 items)** — even though her PR-AUC standalone (0.7084) is below fusion and diana. Her ranking promotes items that all three of the others under-rank, which is the diversity contribution that pushes the 4-way blends past every 3-way blend. With albina, blends finally recover fusion's R@P≥0.9 instead of degrading it. **Role: top-of-ranking diversifier and R@P recoverer.**

## 10. What we did not do (deliberate)

- **Trained meta-classifier (LR/LGBM over probas).** Would require per-domain probas on `team_val_idx.npy`. Team decided to ship naive linear blending with bootstrap CIs as the final method — see section 9 of the 3-domain report for the full rationale.
- **Per-weight grid search optimised on test.** We hand-picked weights informed by the previous pilots; bootstrap CI accounts for sampling variance but does not formally correct for blend selection.
- **Calibration (isotonic / Platt) before blending.** Marginal effect estimated by Diana at ~0.001 PR-AUC, well below the bootstrap CI width.
- **Per-domain breakdown of metrics.** Future work for the individual chapters — measuring how each model performs specifically on items from its own native domain in the team test set.

## 12. Ready formulation for the shared thesis chapter

> We combine four independently trained domain-specialist models — Sonya's fintech fusion (CLIP + e5-text + tabular), Karina's mobile/ads CatBoost, Diana's real-estate M2 multimodal CatBoost, and Albina's headline model — via naive linear blending on a team-wide test split (n = 58,410, base rate 7.95%). Cross-domain Pearson correlations sit in the 0.84 – 0.93 range, well below the ~0.98 we observe within a single author's model family, leaving substantial headroom for ensembling.
>
> The weighted blend **0.35·Sonya + 0.15·Karina + 0.30·Diana + 0.20·Albina** improves PR-AUC from 0.7284 (Sonya alone) to **0.7459 (Δ = +1.75 pp, 95% CI [+1.19, +2.32])** and ROC-AUC from 0.9522 to **0.9599 (Δ = +0.76 pp, [+0.66, +0.87])**, both statistically significant under paired bootstrap with B = 1000. Each successive added domain produced a marginal gain: +0.60 pp PR-AUC for 2 domains, +1.46 pp for 3, +1.75 pp for 4.
>
> At the top-K = 500 audit list, the union of the four models flags **887 true counterfeits vs 452 for Sonya alone — a +96.2% increase in coverage at a fixed analyst budget.** Each author contributes a distinct number of items uniquely caught at top-500: Albina 140, Sonya 120, Karina 96, Diana 66; no single domain dominates the marginal coverage, confirming that the ensemble's strength is structural rather than driven by a single strongest model.
>
> We restrict our evaluation to naive linear blending with paired bootstrap CIs; a trained stacking meta-classifier was excluded by design because it would require per-domain validation probas, which were unavailable within the team-deadline window.
